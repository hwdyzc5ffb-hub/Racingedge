Key Contenders
Bow EchoFAV
7/2
Betfred
Trainer: G. Boughey
Jockey: B. Loughnane
Form: 1-1-1
Unbeaten in three starts and trained right on the Newmarket doorstep, Bow Echo goes in off a spring his trainer describes as “totally faultless.” Skipped a trial in favour of racecourse gallops on the Rowley Mile and was seen quickening sharply through the famous Dip. His sire Night Of Thunder won this very race in 2014.
“We fear everyone. It's the Guineas. But I wouldn't swap him.”
— George Boughey, Trainer
“He's the full package — brilliant jockey, great mind. I consider him a friend. That gives him the freedom to ride with complete confidence.”
— George Boughey, on Billy Loughnane
💛 Bow Echo runs in the yellow and black spots of his late breeder Sheikh Mohammed Obaid, who passed away in December 2025. Winning this Classic would be a deeply poignant tribute.

Distant Storm

Betfred
Trainer: C. Appleby
Jockey: W. Buick
Godolphin
Godolphin are gunning for a third consecutive Guineas victory. Electric when winning the Tattersalls Stakes at this track last September, then a solid third in the Dewhurst. Appleby has kept him fresh, believing he shows up best on the day.
“He's a very clean-winded horse. We are lucky enough to go straight into a Guineas. He's a better horse running fresh.”
— Charlie Appleby, Trainer
Gstaad
9/2
Betfred
Trainer: A. O'Brien
Jockey: R. Moore
37% bet share
The most popular ante-post bet this week. Won the Coventry Stakes at Royal Ascot, then overcame trouble in running to land the Breeders' Cup Juvenile Turf. Connections paid £30,000 to supplement him back in after an admin error saw him mistakenly scratched. Ryan Moore takes the ride.
⚡ Drama alert: Gstaad was accidentally scratched last month. O'Brien paid £30,000 to get him back in — a clear sign of confidence from Ballydoyle.
Oxagon

The Craven Stakes winner two weeks ago — on this exact course and distance. Fitted with first-time cheekpieces, he dictated from the front and kicked impressively through the Dip. The Gosdens are still chasing their elusive first 2000 Guineas and Oxagon gives them their best chance in years.
“He's the perfect model — good mover, nice temperament, very professional today.”

RacingEdge Verdict
🎯
The RacingEdge Call

Bow Echo is the rightful favourite — unbeaten, course-proven, trained by a man who has been here before. The fast ground forecast suits him perfectly.

Gstaad with Ryan Moore is a solid each-way play at 9/2. The highest-rated horse in the field and Moore rarely boards a supplementary runner without expecting to go close.

Our NAP is Oxagon at 11/1. Same track, same trip, won with real authority just two weeks ago under a masterclass from Oisin Murphy. The Gosdens know this course better than anyone. The each-way terms make this a very appealing bet.
