🏆 Epsom & Irish Derby
4yo colt
The dual Derby champion makes his seasonal reappearance at the very track where last year’s story began. Twelve months ago he won the Chester Vase here “going away” — a performance that marked him out as a thorough stayer and set him on the path to Epsom. He won the Derby three and three-quarter lengths clear, then backed it up in the Irish Derby. Now he drops back to 10½ furlongs and wears cheekpieces for the first time to sharpen him up on his return.
“He’s a very straightforward horse. He loved this track, the tight turns suit him perfectly. We’re very much looking forward to seeing him back.”
— Aidan O’Brien, Trainer
“I knew we’d gone a good gallop but Lambourn’s ears were pricked so I knew he had plenty left. He’s a horse we always felt would stay.”
— Wayne Lordan, Jockey — after Epsom Derby 2025
🍀 The RacingEdge angle: Lambourn won the Chester Vase here at 11/8 last May — this is his happy hunting ground. The trip of 1m2½f is shorter than his best, but the cheekpieces suggest O’Brien wants him switched on quickly. He heads entries for the Prince of Wales’s Stakes at Royal Ascot — this is simply a confidence-building reappearance. Back him to do the job.
⭐ Manhattan Stakes bonus: The Huxley winner earns a fees-paid berth in the $1 million Manhattan Stakes at Saratoga, which also serves as a route to the Melbourne Cup. Winning connections will have that firmly in mind.

Trainer: G. Scott
4yo colt
Grp 1 penalty
The chief market rival according to official ratings. Won a Group 1 in Germany by seven lengths last autumn — a stunning performance — and bypassed the Jockey Club Stakes at Newmarket last week when the ground came up too fast. Gets a kinder surface here but does carry a Group 1 penalty of 5lb. The question is whether 1m2½f around the tight Roodee is his optimum trip — connections have hinted he may want further.
⚖️ Watch out: That Group 1 penalty means he concedes weight to Lambourn. The form figures are outstanding but the trip and track could catch him out on the testing Roodee bends.

RacingEdge Verdict
🌿
The RacingEdge Call

Lambourn is our NAP and it isn’t close. The dual Derby winner returns to the exact track where he announced himself last season, and Wayne Lordan — who gave him a perfect front-running ride at Epsom — is back in the saddle. The cheekpieces are a first-time addition that will sharpen him up, and the good ground is ideal. He is simply a class apart from this field.

The only genuine concern is the trip — 1m2½f is shorter than his best. But O’Brien clearly believes he’s good enough to win regardless, and the bonus of a $1 million Manhattan Stakes berth at Saratoga gives connections every reason to have him spot-on.

Bay City Roller is the obvious market rival, but the Group 1 penalty and questions over the Chester trip make him harder to fancy at the price. Royal Rhyme is our each-way selection at 5/1 — if he’s retained his ability, he’s a proven performer at this level.
